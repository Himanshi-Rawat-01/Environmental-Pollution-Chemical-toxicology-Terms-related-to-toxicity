Hazardous Chemicals & Their Impact

Mercury (Hg)

Severe effects on nervous system & kidneys.

Mercury (Hg)

Impact on Health:
Damages the nervous system, leading to tremors, memory loss, and cognitive impairments.
Causes kidney damage and developmental defects in fetuses and children.
Chronic exposure can lead to Minamata disease, characterized by neurological and physical deformities.

Environmental Impact:
Bioaccumulates in aquatic food chains, especially in fish, causing widespread ecological toxicity.
Contaminates soil and water, persisting for decades due to its non-biodegradable nature.

Arsenic (As)

Highly toxic; contaminates groundwater.

Arsenic (As)

Source: Found in groundwater, pesticides, wood preservatives, and industrial processes such as smelting.

Impact on Health:

Long-term exposure causes skin lesions, cancer (skin, lung, bladder), and cardiovascular diseases.
Leads to neurological effects and developmental problems in children.

Environmental Impact:
Contaminates soil and water, affecting crops and entering the food chain.
Highly mobile in groundwater, posing a major risk to human populations in arsenic-prone regions.

Cadmium (Cd)

Causes lung, liver, and kidney damage.

Cadmium (Cd)

Source: Cadmium is released from battery production, electroplating, pigment manufacturing, and fossil fuel combustion.

Impact on Health:

Causes kidney damage and bone demineralization (Itai-Itai disease).
Can lead to lung and prostate cancer on long-term inhalation exposure.

Environmental Impact:

Accumulates in agricultural soil and crops, especially rice.
Highly toxic to aquatic life, affecting reproduction and growth of fish and invertebrates.

Chromium (Cr)

Hexavalent chromium is highly carcinogenic.

Chromium (Cr)

Source: Chromium is widely used in stainless steel manufacturing, tanning, electroplating, and dye industries.

Impact on Health:

Hexavalent chromium [Cr(VI)] is highly toxic, causing respiratory problems, skin ulcers, and lung cancer.
Prolonged exposure can damage kidneys and liver.


Environmental Impact:
Pollutes water bodies, affecting aquatic ecosystems.
Persistent in soil, potentially entering the food chain through crops.

Lead (Pb)

Neurotoxin affecting brain and blood systems.

Lead (Pb)

Source: Lead is found in old paints, batteries, gasoline, plumbing, and some industrial emissions.

Impact on Health:

Neurotoxic, affecting brain development in children, leading to reduced IQ and behavioral issues.
Causes anemia, kidney damage, and hypertension in adults.

Environmental Impact:

Accumulates in soil and sediments, persisting for decades.
Toxic to wildlife, causing reproductive and developmental issues in birds and mammals.
